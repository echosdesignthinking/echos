"The mind that opens up to a new idea never returns to its original size."

Albert Einstein

Design Thinking is a practical and human centred approach that accelerates innovation and solves complex problems. To understand design thinking and start working or living using this mindset, we need to internalise three core values – EMPATHY, COLLABORATION and EXPERIMENTATION.

Design thinking helps you change your mindset and the way you make your decisions through human centred lenses. You will learn to envision opportunities through the identification of real human needs and to question the toughest questions. You will learn to build to think and ultimately you become an innovator that is able to face risks and learn from mistakes that can lead you to real impactful solutions

As a mindset, design thinking enables you to design solutions that are desirable. By testing and iterating solutions, you can deliver appropriate, actionable, and tangible strategies through three different lenses: feasibility, viability and desirability. The solutions that emerge at the end of the Human-Centered Design process should hit the overlap of these three lenses generating real innovation.

The Experience

This highly energetic 3-day program will give you a common level of understanding on the Design Thinking principles, key tools & terminology. The focus of this course will be hands-on following exactly what we believe in learning by doing.

Our program is based on the three main values of Design Thinking: empathy, collaboration and experimentation and it goes beyond. During the three-day program, students complete two real projects using the approach.

What you'll learn

  • How to successfully innovate after going through two real Design Thinking projects;
  • A mindset to solve complex problems;
  • Pratical tools to become empathic, to colaborate and to prototype any idea.

How you'll feel

  • Better equipped to collaborate effectively in a diversified group of participants;
  • Better equipped to learn from your internal and external clients and to quickly prototype in order to generate innovative solutions;
  • Confidence in your creative skills;
  • Confidence to apply the key tools to solve any challenge you may face.

Design Thinking Values

To understand Design Thinking and start working or living under its mental model, we need to internalise three fundamental values: empathy, collaboration and experimentation.

Internalising these three values means changing mental and working models, because it forces us to get up from our chairs, go out on the streets, work in groups, co-create, listen to each other, build upon someone else’s idea and, of course, experiment and take risks.
